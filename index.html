Introduction
Problem

The Tonal Weakness Barrier

Neural audio codecs outperform legacy codecs at low bitrates, but they consistently fail on sustained harmonic content such as bells, glockenspiel, triangles, and long piano notes.

Observed Artifacts
  • Pitch instability: wavering notes where stability is expected
  • Metallic artifacts: harsh, clangy timbre in reconstructions
  • Premature decay: resonance tails collapse too early
  • Reduced musical realism in high-fidelity contexts
Why This Matters
  • Major blocker for high-quality music streaming adoption
  • Limits near-lossless delivery at lower bandwidth cost
  • Prevents NACs from replacing traditional codecs end-to-end
  • Creates a gap between objective and perceived quality

Evolution of Neural Audio Codecs

Project Chimera builds on prior RVQ-based systems and addresses the tonal limitations left unresolved by existing models.

Foundational Systems
  • SoundStream: established convolutional encoder-decoder + RVQ
  • EnCodec: multi-scale STFT discriminators and token LM compression
  • DAC: efficient RVQGAN and factorized codebook lookup
  • Lyra v2: ultra-low bitrate speech focus with WaveGRU
Key Gap Across Prior Work
  • Strong performance on general audio, weaker tonal reconstruction
  • Pitch-stable sustained harmonics remain under-modeled
  • Speech-specialized designs do not solve high-fidelity music quality
  • Need for targeted training data and curriculum control

Methodology

Project Chimera Architecture

An RVQGAN-style 48kHz system with encoder-decoder symmetry and discriminator design tailored to tonal fidelity.

Core Network
  • Encoder: four strided conv blocks with strides 2, 4, 8, 8
  • Latent: 1024-dimensional representation at 93.75 fps
  • RVQ: Nq = 32 codebooks (scalable 16-32), 1024 entries each
  • Decoder: mirrored transposed-convolution reconstruction path
Discriminator Ensemble
  • Periodic discriminator at periods {2,3,5,7,11}
  • Targets pitch wobble and tonal periodicity artifacts
  • Aperiodic multi-resolution STFT discriminator
  • Penalizes metallic noise and harmonic mismatch

Training Objective

Losses and Synthetic Tonal Generation

Chimera uses a composite loss and an on-the-fly generator to force robust modeling of tonal physics.

Total Loss Components
  • L_mel: multi-scale mel-spectrogram reconstruction
  • L_adv_feat and L_adv_gen: adversarial realism pressure
  • L_commit: codebook commitment and stabilization
  • Weighted combination optimized for fidelity + robustness
L_total = w1*L_mel + w2*L_adv_feat + w3*L_adv_gen + w4*L_commit
Synthetic Tonal Signal Engine
  • Randomized fundamental frequencies per event
  • Variable harmonic count with realistic amplitude falloff
  • Randomized decay envelopes for resonance behavior
  • Poisson-timed events for isolated tones and polyphony

Training Strategy

Balanced Curriculum and Phased Training

Training emphasizes tonal learning without degrading general audio capability through controlled sampling and staged optimization.

Data and Sampling Plan
  • 720-hour 48kHz training dataset
  • Per mini-batch: 33% synthetic tonal signals
  • Per mini-batch: 20% isolated instrument stems
  • Remaining samples from broad real-world audio
Two-Phase Schedule
Warm-up (50k steps)
Reconstruction-only to stabilize codebooks
Full Training (350k steps)
All losses active with AdamW + LR decay
Expected Effect
Target tonal gains without catastrophic forgetting

Dual Utility, Contributions, and Limits

Chimera is proposed as both a stronger codec and a better embedding source for automatic audio quality assessment.

Embedding Hypothesis
  • Projected FAD Spearman correlation: 0.82
  • EnCodec baseline correlation: 0.66
  • DAC baseline correlation: 0.81
  • Approaches specialist embedding quality without separate model
Estimated Contribution Stack
  • Baseline DAC: 3.95 MOS
  • + 48kHz operation: +0.07
  • + synthetic tonal data: +0.26 (largest gain)
  • + balanced sampling + EMA codebooks: final 4.42
Anticipated Limitations
  • Higher training and inference compute cost at 48kHz
  • Synthetic data cannot fully match every acoustic nuance
  • Initial focus is weighted toward Western tonal traditions
Future Directions
  • Hierarchical RVQ for more scalable bitrate control
  • Explicit F0-consistency loss for pitch stabilization
  • Contrastive pretraining to close gap with CLAP-class models
